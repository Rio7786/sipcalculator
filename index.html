Frequently Asked Questions

How is the growth of investment calculated for SIPs? +

The formula for calculating the future value of a SIP is:

FV = P × { [(1 + r)ⁿ − 1] / r } × (1 + r)

Where:
FV = Future value of your investment
P = Periodic investment amount
r = Expected annual return ÷ frequency
n = Total number of investment periods


Example: Invest ₹5,000 every month for 40 years at 12% p.a. → portfolio grows to approximately ₹5,94,12,101.

Is SIP better than a Lumpsum investment? +

Both strategies have their merits. SIPs benefit from rupee cost averaging — you automatically buy more units when markets are lower and fewer when they are higher, reducing your average cost over time. Lumpsum works best when markets are near a cyclical low. For most retail investors, SIP is recommended for its built-in discipline and reduced market-timing risk.
